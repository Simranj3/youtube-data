An in-depth analysis of trending videos from Youtube across countries such as Canada, America, France, Germany and Great Britain. This data is taken from Kaggle , which was scraped from the Youtube API.



There are a lot of ways we tend to entertain ourselves. On one end, we might purchase tickets to an immersive experience like Disneyland or other theme parks, and on the other end, we spend hours and hours scrolling through our algorithmic feeds. Youtube has given rise to another form of media that could bridge the two. It's a platform that offers videos that were shorter and more gimmicky to videos of higher quality and production value.

With the help of data visualization, we are able to take a quick peek at some of the trends around trending Youtube videos.



Trends by Country

Let's start by sorting the data by country. The dataset provided a location tag for each Youtube video (i.e: the video went viral in Canada). It should be noted that some data points may overlap (some videos were trending in more than one country).

In this chart, we took all of the trending videos on Youtube and sorted them by the category they were listed under and the country they were trending in. The totals represent the number of videos that were trending in each of the categories.

Scatter Plot Tings

For the following analysis, we decided to focus only on the videos trending in the USA. Let's start with how you should read the following graph:

The Axis

Views
Percent Likes

The x-axis indicates the number of views that an individual video has, while the y-axis shows how many of the total likes/dislikes were likes. This gives us a general feel for how well received a video was.

The Popular Videos

Views
Percent Likes

The top two quadrants are videos that are generally more well received. The right-most quadrant tells us that a video had the most views and was the most well received.

The Unpopular Videos

Views
Percent Likes

The bottom quadrants are videos that are more unpopular, since they have a decreased 'percent like'. The bottom right most video tells us the video with the least number of likes but most views, making it more controversial.
